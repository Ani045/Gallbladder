Understanding Hernia: Causes, Symptoms & Treatment

What is Hernia?

A hernia occurs when an internal organ or tissue protrudes through a weak point in the surrounding muscle or connective tissue. Common locations for hernias include the groin and upper abdomen.

What causes Hernia?

Several factors can contribute to the development of a hernia:

  • Strenuous physical activity
  • Straining during bowel movements
  • Lifting heavy objects
  • Chronic constipation

Symptoms of Hernia

Be aware of these potential signs:

  • A noticeable bulge or swelling
  • Pain or discomfort when lifting
  • A feeling of pressure at the site
  • Nausea or vomiting

Types of Hernia Surgery

There are two main surgical approaches to hernia repair:

  • Open Surgery: The hernia is repaired through an incision, using sutures or mesh.
  • Laparoscopic Surgery: Minimally invasive technique using specialized instruments.

Why Laparoscopic Surgery?

Laparoscopic surgery offers several advantages:

  • Smaller incisions & minimal scarring
  • Shorter hospital stays
  • Reduced post-operative pain
  • Greater surgical precision
  • Lower risk of infection

Why Not Delay Treatment?

Delaying hernia treatment can lead to:

  • Progressive enlargement
  • Increasing pain and discomfort
  • Risk of hernia strangulation
  • Need for more extensive surgery
  • Potential tumor development
